Light curves

A research project studying the variation of active black holes at the centers of galaxies

Python
Pandas
Bokeh
Astropy
Time series
Image convolution

I extracted light curves (time series) of variable astronomical sources (Active Galactic Nuclei) from their raw images.

We were interested in emission from different wavebands as one was tracing the direct emission of a nuclear component and the other one its reflection. In this study we used  cross-correlation of the light curves, the damping in the reflected component, and simulated light curves for significance check, in order to retrieve additional constraints on the physical properties of the source, like the distance between emitting and reflecting component.

Part of this work was published in a peer reviewed paper (see section 4). See also my PhD thesis, Chapter 4. For the light curve extraction see this notebook and this .py file where the functions used in the notebook are defined.
